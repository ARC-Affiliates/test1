Plumbinig Glossary

Definitions of Plumbinig terms as used by test1 in Tampa, FL. 21 terms defined.

Jump to: A · B · D · E · G · H · L · P · R · S · T · W

A

Anode Rod

An anode rod is a sacrificial metal rod inside a tank water heater that attracts corrosive minerals to protect the tank from rust. In Tampa's hard water, anode rods can degrade in as little as two to three years. Replacing the rod periodically can significantly extend the heater's useful life.

B

Backflow Preventer

A backflow preventer is a device installed on a water line to stop contaminated water from reversing direction and entering the clean water supply. It is required by code in many Florida municipalities for irrigation systems and commercial properties. Annual testing is typically mandated to ensure proper function.

D

Drain Snake

A drain snake, also called an auger, is a flexible coiled cable used to break up or retrieve clogs in pipes. Handheld versions clear minor sink and toilet blockages, while motorized models tackle deeper main line obstructions. It is often the first tool used before considering hydro jetting.

E

Expansion Tank

An expansion tank is a small tank attached to a water heater that absorbs pressure caused by thermal expansion as water heats up. Without one, excess pressure can damage the heater, pipes, or fixtures. Most Florida jurisdictions require expansion tanks on closed plumbing systems with backflow preventers.

G

Galvanized Pipe

Galvanized pipe is steel pipe coated with zinc, commonly used for water supply lines in homes built before the 1960s. Over decades, the interior corrodes and restricts water flow, causing low pressure and discolored water. Replacement with PEX or copper is often recommended once corrosion is widespread.

H

Hydro Jetting

Hydro jetting is a drain cleaning method that uses high-pressure water streams, typically between 1,500 and 4,000 PSI, to scour the inside of pipes. It removes grease, scale, tree roots, and stubborn buildup that traditional snaking cannot fully clear. It is often recommended for recurring clogs in main sewer lines.

L

Lift Station

A lift station is a pumping system that moves wastewater from low-lying areas to higher elevations where gravity flow can resume. They are common on Florida properties with below-grade bathrooms or coastal lots near sea level. Routine maintenance is essential to prevent backups and overflows.

P

P-Trap

A P-trap is the curved section of pipe beneath sinks, tubs, and other fixtures that holds a small amount of water. This water seal blocks sewer gases from entering the home while still allowing wastewater to flow through. A dry or cracked P-trap is a common source of foul bathroom odors.

PEX Piping

PEX, or cross-linked polyethylene, is a flexible plastic piping widely used for residential water supply lines. It resists corrosion, scale, and freeze damage, and installs faster than copper with fewer fittings. Many Florida re-pipe projects use PEX as a durable, cost-effective alternative to copper or CPVC.

Pipe Relining

Pipe relining is a trenchless repair method where a resin-saturated liner is inserted into a damaged pipe and cured to form a new pipe within the old one. This creates a seamless, jointless interior that resists root intrusion and corrosion. It typically lasts 50 years or more without major maintenance.

R

Recirculating Pump

A recirculating pump moves hot water continuously through plumbing lines so it is instantly available at every fixture. This eliminates the wait for hot water and reduces water waste at faucets and showers. Many models include timers or smart controls to operate only during peak usage hours.

S

Sewer Camera Inspection

A sewer camera inspection involves running a waterproof, high-resolution video camera through a drain or sewer line to visually diagnose problems. Technicians can identify cracks, blockages, root intrusion, or pipe collapses without digging. The footage helps homeowners make informed repair decisions and verify the scope of work needed.

Sewer Line Cleanout

A sewer line cleanout is a capped vertical pipe that provides direct access to the main sewer line for inspection and cleaning. It allows plumbers to insert cameras, snakes, or jetters without removing toilets or fixtures. Florida building codes generally require accessible cleanouts on every property.

Slab Leak

A slab leak occurs when a water or sewer pipe beneath a home's concrete foundation develops a leak. Warning signs include warm spots on floors, unexplained water bill increases, and the sound of running water. Detection requires specialized equipment like electronic leak detectors or thermal imaging.

Sump Pump

A sump pump is a submersible pump installed in a pit at the lowest point of a home to remove accumulated groundwater. It activates automatically when water reaches a certain level, discharging it away from the foundation. In flood-prone Florida areas, a working sump pump can prevent costly water damage.

T

Tankless Water Heater

A tankless water heater heats water on demand as it flows through the unit, eliminating the need for a storage tank. These systems offer endless hot water, save space, and can reduce energy bills by 20-30 percent. They are popular in Florida homes where garage temperatures shorten traditional tank lifespans.

Toilet Flange

A toilet flange is the circular fitting that secures a toilet to the floor and connects it to the drainpipe. A damaged or improperly seated flange can cause rocking toilets, leaks at the base, and sewer gas odors. Repairing or replacing it is often necessary during bathroom remodels or after water damage.

Trenchless Pipe Repair

Trenchless pipe repair replaces or restores damaged underground pipes without extensive excavation. Techniques like pipe bursting and cured-in-place pipe (CIPP) lining minimize damage to lawns, driveways, and landscaping. This approach is often faster and less disruptive than traditional dig-and-replace methods.

W

Water Pressure Regulator

A water pressure regulator, or PRV, is a valve that reduces incoming municipal water pressure to a safe level for home plumbing, typically 50-70 PSI. Excessive pressure can damage fixtures, appliances, and pipe joints over time. Failing regulators often cause banging pipes, leaks, or premature appliance wear.

Water Softener

A water softener removes calcium and magnesium ions from hard water through an ion exchange process. In the Tampa Bay area, where groundwater is notoriously hard, softeners help prevent scale buildup in pipes, appliances, and water heaters. They also improve soap lathering and reduce spotting on dishes.

Whole House Repipe

A whole house repipe is the complete replacement of a home's water supply lines, typically with PEX or copper. It is recommended when pipes are leaking in multiple locations, made of failing materials like polybutylene, or causing chronically low pressure. Most projects are completed in two to five days.
