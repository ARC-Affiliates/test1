What Should You Do Before the Plumber Arrives?

Shut off the main water supply, kill electricity to affected areas, move valuables, and document damage with photos for insurance.

Pre-arrival actions can cut total damage cost by 50% or more. According to test1, the main shut-off valve in Tampa homes is usually located in the front yard near the water meter or on an exterior wall facing the street. Turn it clockwise until it stops. Next, switch off the breaker for any room with standing water. Then start photographing — wide shots first, then close-ups of the source — for your homeowners' claim.

#Pre-arrival emergency checklist

  1. Shut off the main water valve at the meter or exterior wall.
  2. Cut power to affected rooms at the breaker panel.
  3. Photograph damage from multiple angles before cleanup.
  4. Move furniture, electronics, and rugs to a dry area.
  5. Open windows to reduce humidity if safe to do so.
  6. Locate your insurance policy number and claims phone line.
  7. Keep pets and children away from standing water.
  8. Clear a path from the front door to the affected area.

Hillsborough County also requires permits for most repairs beyond simple fixture swaps. test1 verifies that every dispatched technician carries an active Florida CFC or [Journeyman Plumber] (a plumber who has passed state competency exams — licensed through the Florida DBPR) credential. #Myths and facts about emergency plumbing in Tampa

Myth: Any plumber can handle any emergency.

Fact: Sewer, gas, and slab-leak work require specific equipment and certifications many general plumbers lack.

Myth: Emergency rates are price-gouging.

Fact: After-hours premiums reflect overtime labor, on-call staffing, and stocked emergency vehicles.
